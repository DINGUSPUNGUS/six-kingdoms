Landscaping in Plettenberg Bay: Luxury Ecological Gardens & EcoPools

Plettenberg Bay is one of South Africa's most sought-after coastal destinations, with a property market that demands gardens of genuine quality. Ecological landscaping in Plett means creating spaces that match the extraordinary setting — and that age well in a sensitive coastal environment.

Six Kingdoms works with private homeowners, estate developers, and holiday property managers across Plettenberg Bay and its surrounds including Keurboom Strand, The Crags, Harkerville, and Natures Valley. Our work spans ecological garden design, natural swimming pool installation, and invasive plant management.

Plettenberg Bay's Unique Ecological Context

Plett sits at the confluence of the Bitou and Keurbooms rivers, which drain into a large estuary system that is one of the most significant estuarine habitats on the southern Cape coast. The surrounding landscape includes coastal fynbos, afrotemperate forest pockets, and coastal thicket — all of which are species-rich and ecologically sensitive.

Coastal properties in Plett face specific challenges: salt-laden winds off the Indian Ocean, sandy soils with low fertility, and exposure to strong southerly and southwesterly weather systems. Inland properties are more sheltered but often carry heavier invasive plant loads. A good ecological garden design in Plett accounts for these site-specific pressures from the start.

The area is also ecologically important in its own right. Properties adjoining the Bitou River corridor, the Garden Route National Park, or CapeNature reserves carry specific obligations and opportunities. Six Kingdoms is experienced in designing gardens on sensitive land — ones that contribute to the surrounding ecology rather than detracting from it.

Ecological Garden Design for Plett Properties

In Plettenberg Bay, garden design for high-value properties means three things: it must look exceptional, it must function well in a coastal environment, and it must require manageable maintenance given that many properties are used seasonally. Indigenous planting addresses all three requirements simultaneously.

Our Plett garden designs typically include:

  • Structural coastal fynbos planting — Proteas, Leucadendrons, and Restios that thrive in wind exposure and poor, well-drained soils
  • Evergreen screening with locally-adapted species like Wild olive (Olea europaea subsp. africana) and Cape honeysuckle (Tecoma capensis)
  • Pollinator gardens designed to support the unusual coastal fynbos bee species of the eastern Garden Route
  • Natural pools and water features that integrate with the garden without requiring chemical management
  • Low-maintenance groundcover combinations that suppress weeds and hold sandy soil in dry periods

EcoPools in Plettenberg Bay

Natural swimming pools are particularly well-suited to Plettenberg Bay properties. The climate supports vigorous aquatic plant growth, which drives the biological filtration that keeps the water clean and clear. A natural pool in a Plett garden requires no chlorine, no stabilisers, and no salt systems — just the right plant selection and a sound hydraulic design.

Six Kingdoms has installed EcoPools in Plettenberg Bay including the Green House project at Keurboom Strand — a fully integrated natural pool and ecological garden that demonstrates what is possible when pool design and landscape design are considered together from the start. The pool features a generous swimming zone with a deep water point, a regeneration zone of aquatic plants, and a surround of indigenous coastal planting that blurs the boundary between pool and garden.

Land Management Around Plettenberg Bay

The Crags, Harkerville, and properties along the Bitou River valley carry significant invasive plant pressure, particularly Bugweed (Solanum mauritianum), Long-leafed wattle (Acacia longifolia), and Port Jackson willow. These species can quickly dominate a property if left unmanaged, reducing biodiversity and creating fire risk.

Six Kingdoms provides professional invasive plant management across the Plett area, from single-property clearing programmes to multi-year land stewardship agreements for larger holdings. We work in conjunction with the Bitou Municipality's greening initiatives and can advise on responsible disposal and replanting protocols.
