Ecological Design

Case studies from our work across the Garden Route

Ecological design is an approach to landscaping that works with natural processes rather than against them — creating spaces that are beautiful, functional, and genuinely good for the land they sit on.

At Six Kingdoms, we design and build landscapes that support local ecology, conserve natural resources, and produce real abundance. Our work draws on permaculture principles, whole systems thinking, and deep familiarity with the Garden Route environment to integrate indigenous gardens, food-producing systems, and sustainable water management into cohesive, living landscapes.

The result is outdoor space that looks after itself, supports local wildlife, and rewards the people who use it — year after year.

A mix of what we do — pools, land, food gardens, and a couple of workshops that turned into something bigger.
