Sine's Back Yard Natural Pool

Knysna, Garden Route — New Build

Type Natural Dipping Pool
Location Knysna, Garden Route
Site size Small residential
Build type New build
Chemicals used None. Ever.

Sine had a back yard that looked like it couldn't take a pool. The space was tight, the slope was awkward, and her primary concern was maintenance — she wanted to swim, not spend her weekends managing chemistry. What she ended up with was a compact natural dipping pool that required less looking after than the garden bed beside it.

The Challenge

The site constraints were real. A small Knysna back yard with a slope to work with, neighbouring trees casting shade across part of the space, and a client who was clear about one thing: no chemicals, no complexity, no fuss. The question was whether the biology could do its job effectively in a pool of this size.

Smaller natural pools present a slightly different engineering challenge to larger ones. The ratio of swimming volume to regeneration zone needs more careful calibration — too little planted zone and the biology becomes unstable; too much and you've given up swimming space. The design worked out a compact but functional split, with the planted zone positioned to receive the most light available on that site.

What We Built

The pool was designed with a clearly defined swim zone and a planted regeneration zone separated by a submerged wall — a clean visual divide that keeps the swimming area looking sharp while the biology does its work next door. Aquatic plants were selected for their ability to handle the partial shade conditions: a mix of sedges, restios adapted to wet feet, and submerged oxygenators that would keep the water clear through summer without additional intervention.

The pool's edges were finished with natural stone that matched the existing garden aesthetic — no jarring tiles, no chlorine-blue surrounds. It was designed to look like it had always been there.

"We had no idea a swimming pool could feel this alive. Six Kingdoms transformed what we imagined as just a pool into something the whole family — and apparently the local frogs — can't get enough of." — Sine M., Knysna

What Happened After

The biology established faster than expected. By the second month, water clarity was stable without any intervention. By summer, the first dragonflies arrived — which, on a natural pool, is the clearest indicator that the water chemistry is genuinely healthy. Frogs followed shortly after.

Sine's experience of the pool is exactly what a well-designed EcoPool should produce: you get in and you don't think about it. The water doesn't smell of anything. It doesn't sting your eyes. You get out and your skin doesn't feel stripped. You just swim.

Key Details

  • Compact design engineered for a challenging, partially shaded small plot
  • Dedicated regeneration zone with shade-tolerant indigenous aquatic plants
  • Natural stone surround integrated with the existing garden character
  • Zero chemicals used — filtration entirely biological from day one
  • Dragonflies and tree frogs established by the end of the first season
