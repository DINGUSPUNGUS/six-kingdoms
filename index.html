About Six Kingdoms

Six Kingdoms is an ecological design practice rooted in South Africa's Garden Route. We build natural swimming pools, design for biodiversity, and steward land back to ecological health.

We read the land, respond to its patterns, and create spaces that sustain themselves — and inspire the people who inhabit them.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about EcoPools, ecological design, and land management

An EcoPool is a natural swimming pool filtered by plants and beneficial micro-organisms instead of chemicals. The result is living, clean water that supports biodiversity, looks beautiful, and is gentler on your body than a conventional chlorinated pool.

Natural swimming pools (EcoPools) use plants and beneficial micro-organisms to filter the water instead of chemicals like chlorine. This creates a healthier swimming environment, supports biodiversity, and requires less maintenance over time. They're also more environmentally friendly and blend beautifully into the landscape.

We're based in South Africa's Garden Route and primarily service this region, including areas around Plettenberg Bay, Knysna, George, and surrounding communities. For larger projects, we may consider traveling further afield.

Ecological design means working with the natural patterns of a site — its soils, water flows, sun angles, and existing biodiversity — to create spaces that thrive without constant intervention. We use native plants, restore degraded areas, and build landscapes that become more resilient over time.

Yes, we provide ongoing land management and stewardship services. Part of our philosophy is helping clients develop a deep connection with their landscapes, so we offer training and support to help you maintain your garden, as well as periodic consultations and hands-on maintenance if needed.

Get in touch via the contact form or email us at info@sixkingdoms.org. We'll arrange an initial conversation to understand your site and goals, then outline the best path forward — whether that's an EcoPool, an ecological design brief, or a land management plan.
